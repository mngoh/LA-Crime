LA Crime Analysis

LAPD data, 2020 – 2023  ·  Focus: assault victimization by demographics and geography

Results Overview

  • In 100,946 assault incidents across Los Angeles (2020–2023), Black women are the only group where female victims outnumber male victims, making up 56–70% of Black assault victims depending on the division.
  • The pattern shows up across 77th Street, Southeast, Southwest, and Newton divisions, which argues against it being a fluke of one area.
  • Hispanic, White, and Asian victims all follow the opposite trend, with male victims making up roughly 55–60% in each group.
  • The gap is sharpest in Southeast, where Black female victims (864) nearly double Black male victims (378).
Limitations: These are reported crimes only. Reporting rates likely differ across groups and areas. All figures are raw victim counts, not rates. Without racial population data at the division level, we can't say whether the disparity reflects a higher victimization rate or simply a higher share of Black women living in these areas. The census data available for this project does not include racial breakdowns by LAPD division, so per-capita analysis was not possible.

Key Findings

Gender disparity unique to Black victims

Black women are assault victims at significantly higher rates than Black men. This pattern does not appear consistently in White, Hispanic, or Asian victim data, making it a demographically distinct finding.

Geographic concentration

The disparity is sharpest in 77th Street, Southeast, and Southwest districts. In the Northeast district, Black women experience over 75% more assaults than Black men in the same area.

Dominant weapon type

The majority of assaults across all groups involve strong-arm force (hands, fists, feet) rather than weapons. Hispanic victims account for the highest single count at 13,783 incidents via strong-arm force.
